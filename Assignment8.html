Theory Work

[1] Consider the finite difference method

v_{m}^{n+1}=v_{m}^{n}-k {\rm a} \;\dfrac{v_{m+1}^{n+1}-v_{m-1}^{n+1}}{2h}

used to create an approximate solution to the equation $v_t + {\rm a} v_x = 0$, $a > 0$, for $x\;\in \; [0, 1], t \; \in \;[0, T]$ , and $v$ periodic.

(a) Is there a CFL restriction associated with this scheme?

(b) Under what condition (if any) is this scheme stable in the 2-norm?

(c) If this scheme is written in matrix form as ${\rm A}v^{n+1}=v^n$, then one can consider using the Sherman-Morrison-Woodbury formula to obtain $v^{n+1}$ from $v^{n}$. If $\rm {A}= {\rm (A}^\prime + {\rm UV}^t \rm{)}$ then this formula is

\rm{A}^{-1} = (\rm{A}^{\prime})^{-1} - (\rm{A}^{\prime})^{-1} \rm{U} [\; \rm{I} + \rm{V}^t (\rm{A}^{\prime})^{-1} \rm{U}\;]^{-1} \rm{V}^t (\rm{A}^{\prime})^{-1}

(i) How should one choose the matrices $\rm{U}$ and $\rm{V}$ so that $\rm{A}^{\prime}$ is tridiagonal?

(ii) Under what conditions on k and h (if any) will insure that tri-diagonal solution technique used for homework #7 will be successful when applied to \rm{A}^{\prime}?

[2] Consider the semi-implicit finite difference method

v^{n+1}=v^{n}-k\;a {\rm D}_{0} v^{n} + k { \rm b}\; {\rm D}_{+} {\rm D}_{-} v^{n+1}

used to create an approximate solution to the equation $v_t + {\rm a} \; v_x = {\rm b} \;v_{xx} $, a and b > 0, for $x\;\in \; [0, 1], t \; \in \;[0, T]$ and $v$ periodic.

(a) What is the amplification factor for this scheme?

(b) Under what condition (if any) is this scheme stable?


[3] (i) Derive the finite difference approximation to $({\rm b}(x) v_x )_x$ using the "finite volume" technique.

(ii) If $x\;\in \; [0, 1]$ and homogeneous Dirichlet boundary conditions are assumed for $v$ , give the matrix representation of this finite difference approximation.




[4] (a) Assume $(x,\;y)\;\in \; [0, 1] \times [0,1]\;$ and consider the differential operator ${ \rm L}v = ( v_{xx} + v_{yy} ) $ with homogenous Dirichlet boundary conditions on $v$ . Show that the functions

s_{(p,q)} (x,y) = \sin(\rm{p} \pi x) \sin (\rm{q} \pi y)

for p = 1,2, 3, ... and q = 1, 2, 3 ... are eigenfunctions of L and give their corresponding eigenvalues.

(b) Give the solution to the equation $v_t = {\rm b} ( v_{xx} + v_{yy})$, b > 0, for $(x,\;y)\;\in \; [0, 1] \times [0,1]\;$ with initial condition $v(x,y,0) =\sin(\rm{p} \pi x) \sin (\rm{q} \pi y)$ for p and q positive integers and homogeneous Dirichlet boundary conditions for $v$.
