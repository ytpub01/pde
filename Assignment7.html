[1] Consider the following identity for an n x n tri-diagonal matrix

\rm \left(
\begin{array}{ccccc}
a_{0} & c_{0} & & & \\
b_{1} & a_{1} & c_{1} & & \\
& \ast & \ast & \ast & \\
& & \ast & \ast & c_{n-2} \\
& & & b_{n-1} & a_{n-1}
\end{array}
\right) =\left(
\begin{array}{ccccc}
1 & & & & \\
d_{1} & 1 & & & \\
& \ast & \ast & & \\
& & \ast & \ast & \\
& & & d_{n-1} & 1
\end{array}
\right) \left(
\begin{array}{ccccc}
e_{0} & c_{0} & & & \\
& e_{1} & c_{1} & & \\
& & \ast & \ast & \\
& & & \ast & c_{n-2} \\
& & & & e_{n-1}
\end{array}
\right)


By equating the matrix elements in the above identity one finds that di and ei are given by

\begin{eqnarray}e_0 &=& a_0 \\
d_{i} &=& \dfrac{b_{i}}{e_{i-1}}\\
e_{i} &=& a_{i} -{d_{i}}*{c_{i-1}} \qquad i = 1,2,\ldots, n-1
\end{eqnarray}

Let A be the matrix on the left hand side of the above identity. Given the coefficients $d_i$ and $e_i$, the solution of the system of equations Ax = z can be found by forward

\begin{eqnarray}y_0 &=& z_0 \\
y_{i} &=& z_{i} -{d_{i}}*{y_{i-1}} \qquad i = 1,2,\ldots, n-1
\end{eqnarray}

and then back substitution

\begin{eqnarray}
x_{n-1} &=& \dfrac{y_{n-1}}{e_{n-1}}\\
x_{i} &=& \dfrac{(y_{i}-c_{i}*x_{i+1})}{e_{i}} \qquad i = n-2,\ldots,1,0
\end{eqnarray}


Create a routine that uses this procedure to solve systems of tri-diagonal equations. Your routine should accept as input the coefficients of the tri-diagonal matrix in three vectors (one for the a's, one for the b's, and one for the c's) and the vector corresponding to the right hand side of the equation. The routine should return the vector x that is the solution to Px = z. Turn in a listing of your routine.
